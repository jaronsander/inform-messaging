# RIP Roy — My Wife Killed My Mascot

**Type:** A (Personal Story / War Story)
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Status:** Ready to Post
**CTA:** Website link (button above post) + comment engagement (walkthrough offer)

---

My wife killed my mascot. She was right.

Every plumbing company needs a mascot, right? Even a Funnel Plumber.

So I built one. His name was Roy (short for ROI). A little cartoon plumber to break up the dryness of RevOps content. Something memorable.

I spent hours in Gemini CLI with Nano Banana learning how to get AI-generated characters to look even remotely consistent across poses (this was harder than it sounds). Wrote a background removal script. Iterated. A lot.

Finally got results I was proud of. Redesigned the whole website around him. Called my wife over to see it.

Her immediate reaction:

"What is that? Please don't use it. It's too gimmicky."

I tried to defend Roy. He's cute! He adds personality! He's memorable!

But after sitting with it... she was 100% right.

Roy was distracting. Too whimsical. Not the energy you want from someone you're trusting to fix your revenue infrastructure.

Sometimes the best brand decision is killing something you spent hours building.

RIP Roy. You were cute, but you didn't make the cut.
