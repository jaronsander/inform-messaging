Caught Boris Wertz (Version One Ventures) at @VanTechJournal last night. Some takeaways that stuck with me.

Speed is the only moat. Not strategy. Not differentiation. Speed. Because with speed comes capital, customers, and data. In a market where every AI company is chasing the same obvious ideas with the same underlying models, execution is the only separator. Insane competitive teams are racing in every category right now. The ones who move fastest win. Anthropic is eating into OpenAI's market share not because of a better pitch deck, but because they ship.

AI can't spot great founders. This one was interesting. Founders are outliers. AI is trained on averages. The less pattern recognition you have at the earliest stages, the less useful the model is. You still need humans who know where they want to play and know the players. Boris was clear on that.

Outcomes are concentrating. The wins will be bigger, but they'll be held by fewer companies and backed by fewer VCs. ARR isn't even the best metric to evaluate what's working anymore. The game is changing shape.

The through-line across all of it is that speed compounds. Hesitation doesn't.

But speed requires fast decisions. And fast decisions require clean data. If your sales and marketing teams are operating on a broken foundation with bad routing, dirty CRM records, and unreliable pipeline numbers, you're not running a speed machine. You're running a leaky one. Every bad data point is a slow decision, a missed signal, or a wasted rep hour. You can't operate at high performance with too much waste in the system. And at scale, small leaks become floods.

The companies moving fastest aren't just hiring faster or spending faster. They're making better decisions faster because they trust the data underneath them.

Great event. Worth the Tuesday evening.
