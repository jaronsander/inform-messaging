Hot take: AI isn't going to consolidate your tech stack.

It's going to blow it apart. (In a good way.)

I see so many SaaS companies building tools looking to replace functions, like SDRs, CS.

Building software is cheap now. So every platform races to become the "all-in-one" solution. Your CRM wants to be your sequencer. Your sequencer wants to be your CRM. Everyone builds everything.

We've seen how this ends. It's called enshittification.

A product you loved becomes a bloated platform that does 40 things at a B- level. HubSpot. Salesforce. Pick your example.

Here's what AI actually changes:
→ Building focused tools is cheaper, so more best-in-breed options exist
→ Integration is cheaper, so plugging them together isn't a nightmare
→ Switching is cheaper, so lock-in loses its teeth

When switching costs drop, the "we do everything" pitch becomes a red flag, not a feature.

High-performance companies won't consolidate onto one platform. They'll pick the best tool for each job, wire them together tightly, and swap components the moment something better hits the market.

The competitive advantage shifts from "which platform are you on" to "how well is your stack wired together."

That plumbing — the data layer, the orchestration, the observability to know whether things are working or breaking — is the infrastructure gap nobody's talking about.

That's the infrastructure I build and am productizing.

Am I wrong? Or is "we do everything" already a red flag you just haven't said out loud yet?
