# PLAYBOOK: Prospecting & Acquisition

**Role:** The Funnel Plumber  
**Goal:** Identify high-value targets and enter via the "Trojan Horse" strategy.  
**Use When:** You are looking for new business or sending cold outreach.

---

## 1. The Strategy: "The Trojan Horse"

**The Concept:** Companies hiring for "RevOps Managers" or "CRM Admins" are in pain. They know their data is messy.
**The Pivot:** Do NOT apply for the job. Pitch the **CRM & CS Reactivation.**
**The Logic:** "There's hidden pipeline buried in your CRM and CS data right now. Let me surface it before your new hire spends their first quarter hunting it down."

### Triggers & Keywords
Search Job Boards (LinkedIn, Indeed) for:
- "Clean up"
- "Migration" / "Implementation"
- "Data Hygiene"
- "Messy CRM"
- "Salesforce" / "HubSpot"

Track Investment Activity (Pitchbook, Crunchbase, LinkedIn news) for:
- Recent PE acquisitions (60–90 days post-close, before first board reporting cycle)
- Companies announcing fundraise or "exploring strategic options"
- Post-acquisition hiring signals: Chief of Staff, VP Strategy, or RevOps Manager at a newly acquired company

## 2. Outreach Style Guide

**Tone:** Conversational, friendly, confident. Not aggressive, not salesy.
**Length:** 4 sentences for cold DM or email. Slack or warm channels allow 4 to 5 sentences given the more conversational context.
**Rules:**
- No self-introductions or positioning ("I'm a Computer Engineer", "I'm not applying")
- No internal jargon thrown at the prospect (no "reactivation scan", "orphaned leads", "broken schemas")
- No dashes of any kind in messages. No em dashes, no en dashes, no hyphens used as punctuation. Use commas or periods instead.
- Reference their specific pain from the JD naturally, not as a bullet list
- CTA is casual ("Worth a quick chat?" not "Open to a 15-min forensic review of your current stack?")
- Let your profile and prior conversation do the credibility work

**The Two-Message Rule (LinkedIn cold outreach):**
Never pitch in the first message. The first message is pure human connection. Reference something specific: a post they made, a mutual connection, a company they mentioned, a market they operate in. No ask, no setup, no bridge to a pitch. Just a real observation. The pitch goes in the second message, sent after they respond or after a natural pause. This is what earns the read.

Example first message: "Hey [Name]! Thanks for the connect, found you through [specific post or mutual]. [One genuine observation about the content or person]."

The second message is then the pitch using the appropriate template below.

**Two distinct modes depending on what you are selling:**

Sales Mode (finished service): Lead with the ROI guarantee. "$100K in recoverable pipeline or you don't pay." This is the anchor.

Customer Discovery Mode (prototype or early stage): Do NOT lead with the guarantee. The ask is feedback and validation, not a sale. If they are willing to pay, that is a strong signal but not the goal. See Template C.

## 3. Outreach Templates

### Template A: The Job Posting Hook (LinkedIn/Email)
*Use when you've spotted a RevOps/CRM/Sales Ops hire and have already connected or exchanged messages.*

"Also, I saw you're hiring a [Job Title]. I help businesses like [Company] recover hidden pipeline buried in their CRM and GTM stack, especially when there's [1-2 pain signals from JD: e.g., data hygiene debt and Salesforce automation issues] piling up, which it sounds like they are from the posting. If I can't find at least $100K in recoverable pipeline, you don't pay. Worth a quick look?"

### Template C: Customer Discovery / Early Adopter (Slack or Warm Channel)
*Use when you have a prototype and are validating the problem. You want feedback and early adopters, not paying customers (yet). Use in Slack, warm intro channels, or anywhere you have some existing context with the person.*

**Key framing principles:**
- Do not assume the prospect's company already has the problem. Frame from your own observation ("In my experience..."), not a claim about their specific system. This removes any implied blame and keeps it open.
- Reframe data drift as inevitable and systemic, not a people failure. "No amount of manual attention keeps records consistent over time" signals that this is a structural problem, not a competence problem.
- A one-line analogy collapses the concept instantly for tech-literate audiences. "DataDog for RevOps" or "CI/CD for your CRM" work well depending on the audience. DataDog is more universally understood as a monitoring layer.
- The ask is light: would they be open to testing it and sharing feedback?
- Open the referral door. If they are not the ICP, they may still know someone who is. Add "or anyone you know" to the CTA so the conversation has somewhere to go even when they say no to themselves. Do not force it, just leave it open.

"Hey [Name], I noticed [Company] is hiring [Role] and one of the requirements is [specific CRM or data language from the JD]. In my experience, no amount of manual attention keeps all the records consistent over time. That's exactly why I'm building what I think of as DataDog for RevOps, a monitoring layer that catches and flags that drift automatically, and I'm looking for early adopters to test it and give me feedback. Would [Company] be open to taking a look, or [secondary angle if relevant]?"

### Template D: The "What Does It Do?" Response (Any Channel)
*Use when a prospect asks for clarification on what the tool or service actually does. This is the most common friction point. Answer it directly, in plain English, with a concrete mechanism and a soft money hook. No jargon, no positioning, no credentials.*

**The formula:** Cause of the problem (plain English) + What gets found (concrete things, not categories) + Where the money is hiding (range, not guarantee) + Low-friction CTA (walkthrough, not a call).

"The short version: it connects to your CRM and runs a forensic analysis to find pipeline that silently got buried. Orphaned leads, contacts stuck in dead lifecycle stages, dormant accounts that never got followed up because a sync broke or someone left and their deals went nowhere.

It surfaces those as a prioritized list with estimated value, and flags the specific point where each one fell through the cracks.

Most CRMs have $50K to $200K sitting in there that nobody is looking at. Not because deals are dead, but because the system stopped routing them. This finds it.

Happy to do a quick walkthrough if you want to see what it looks like on a real CRM."

**Notes:**
- Use the $50K to $200K range (not the $100K guarantee) when in discovery mode or when the relationship is early. The range is softer and more credible than a guarantee to someone who hasn't asked for one.
- "A quick walkthrough" is a lower commitment ask than "a call" or "a demo." Use it when you want to reduce friction.
- Swap in their specific context if you have it: "orphaned leads from your last integration" is stronger than the generic list.

### Template B: The "Interview" Pivot (Verbal)
*Use when you are on a call that feels like an interview.*

**Client:** "Tell us about your background. Why do you want this job?"

**You:** "To be honest, [Name], I don't think I'm the employee you need right now. I think you need a 'Fixer.'

If you hire a manager now, they’ll spend their first 6 months drowning in the cleanup work you described.

My proposal: Let me come in as a tactical consultant for 3 weeks. I'll run a reactivation scan on your CRM and CS data, surface the buried pipeline, and hand you a clean stack. Then, you hire the manager to drive the car I just fixed. It's faster, cheaper, and gets you immediate revenue."

### Template E: The PE / M&A Outreach
*Use when targeting a recently PE-acquired company or a company preparing for exit or fundraise. These are mandate-driven buyers — urgency comes from investor reporting or due diligence timelines, not pain awareness.*

**For PE portfolio company (direct to RevOps/CRM admin):**
"Hey [Name], noticed [Company] recently went through the [PE firm] acquisition. In my experience, the first board cycle after an acquisition is when pipeline visibility matters most and also when data gaps are most likely to surface from the transition. I help companies in this window run a fast diagnostic to surface hidden pipeline and get data clean for reporting. Worth a quick look?"

**For PE Operating Partner (portfolio-wide pitch):**
"Hey [Name], I work with PE-backed B2B companies on CRM diagnostics post-acquisition. The pattern is consistent: acquisitions always surface data debt, and the first board cycle is when it's most costly to have gaps. I run a 2-week diagnostic per portfolio company, surface the hidden pipeline, and hand over a clean baseline for reporting. Happy to walk you through how it works across a portfolio."

## 4. Qualification Checklist

**Must-Haves (The Green Lights):**
- [ ] B2B SaaS, Platform + Services hybrid, or Tech-Enabled Service (especially with subscription/upsell components).
- [ ] $5M+ ARR (Series B+).
- [ ] Actively Hiring RevOps/Sales roles (Signals pain/growth).
- [ ] Using Salesforce, HubSpot, or Attio + 5-10 other tools.

**OR — Transitional Buyer (skip standard filters):**
- [ ] Recently acquired by PE firm — mandate buyer, board reporting urgency.
- [ ] Actively preparing for fundraise or exit — motivated by valuation impact and due diligence timeline.

**Red Flags (Walk Away):**
- [ ] "Can you just build us a custom app?" (We are infrastructure, not dev shops).
- [ ] "We just need some strategy advice." (No implementation budget).
- [ ] Budget <$4k.

## 5. Competitive Context (Quick Reference)

*When a prospect mentions any of these during outreach, use this to reframe.*

**If they mention Gong/Clari/People.ai:** They think revenue intelligence = RevOps. It's not. These tools analyze conversations and forecast deals. They sit on top of CRM data and assume it's accurate. Our angle: "Those tools are great at what they do, but they depend on clean data underneath. That's what I fix."

**If they mention WeFlow/Scratchpad/Rattle:** They think CRM adoption tools = data quality. These tools make it easier for reps to update Salesforce. Our angle: "That solves the last inch. Your problems are structural. Lifecycle stages, routing logic, integration failures. Different layer entirely."

**If they mention Openprise/Syncari/LeanData:** They're tool-shopping for a data solution. These are the closest software competitors but still require someone to diagnose, configure, and monitor. Our angle: "Tools don't diagnose problems. Engineers do. You might end up using one of those, but someone has to figure out what's actually broken first."

**If they mention a RevOps agency (RevPartners, Go Nimbly, etc.):** They're comparison shopping services. Most agencies are HubSpot-locked and generalist. Our angle: "We specialize in data forensics and ongoing observability. Most agencies do project work and hand off. I build telemetry so it stays fixed."

**If they mention AI cleaning/AI data quality:** The biggest objection in 2026. Every platform is selling "AI handles data hygiene." Our angle: "Their AI cleans what reps enter. Activity capture, field updates, dedup. It doesn't fix structural problems: broken lifecycle stages, dead routing queues, silent integration failures. AI on broken architecture just automates the problems faster."

**If they mention Stack Moxie:** They're thinking about workflow testing and operational uptime. Our angle: "Stack Moxie answers 'did the workflow run?' I answer 'did the workflow drive revenue?' Operational monitoring vs. business efficiency monitoring. Different layer."

## 6. PE & M&A Targeting Strategy

### The Core Insight
PE-backed companies and pre-exit companies are **mandate-driven buyers**. They don't need to be convinced clean data is valuable — their investors require it. The goal is to reach the source of the mandate and position the diagnostic as the fastest path to compliance.

### The PE Operating Partner Play
PE operating partners own the RevOps function across 20–50 portfolio companies. One relationship with an operating partner unlocks portfolio-wide access — each acquisition in their portfolio becomes a qualified opportunity.

**How to find them:**
- LinkedIn: "[PE Firm name] Operating Partner" filtered by GTM/Revenue/Operations function
- PE firm websites list operating partners by functional area
- Portfolio company job boards often name the operating sponsor in the RevOps or CRM role posting

**The portfolio pitch logic:**
Position the diagnostic as a repeatable acquisition protocol: standardized, fast, budget-able at the portfolio level. Frame it as de-risking investor reporting and setting a clean baseline — not as selling a service to each portfolio company individually.

### The Pre-Exit Pitch Logic
For companies preparing for fundraise or sale, frame around valuation impact:
- Hidden pipeline surfaced before due diligence = higher reported ARR = higher multiple
- Data debt found by acquirer during due diligence = valuation discount
- The diagnostic protects the upside and removes the downside risk simultaneously

**The one-liner:** "I find the pipeline you'll lose in due diligence and the data debt that will discount your valuation."
